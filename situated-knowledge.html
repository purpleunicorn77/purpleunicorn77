Situated Knowledge


🦚 MA Project: “Situated knowledges require that the object of knowledge be pictured as an actor and agent, not as a screen or a ground or a resource.” (Haraway, 1988, P.592) In this project, I represented my own experience as a human being and immersed myself in a reflective process.

🦚 Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Figma, 3D Workshop

🦚 Here, I highlight my creative expertise in using hands-on tools and crafting compelling video edits.



🦚 Thinking Process 🦚

I live in one of the world's largest cities — a place driven by consumerism and capitalism. I imagine myself at the center of it all, as if the world revolves within a luxury department store, layered like a cake.






🦚 Reflection 🦚

1. What does capitalism bring to society?

2. How does it affect our lives and culture?

3. What are the politics in consumer capitalism?


🦚 Feedback 🦚

I should do more research on capitalism.

🌿 Conclusion 🌿

Green is the elusive color is our topic. We were not picking a side of whether the green transition is good or not. We only hope the government, companies, and the indigenous people can find the balance. Renewable energy is a great intention to save our earth, but exploiting aboriginal livelihood is not the best solution. Moving onshore wind farms to offshore wind farms may be a better option. However, regulating the offshore route and policy is another problem. Humans are always creating problems and finding solutions. From this project, we were also speaking up for people with fewer resources.
